The Event

Where Young Minds
Shape the World.

Jaipuria Symposium 2026 is a two-day inter-school academic debate symposium hosted by Seth M. R. Jaipuria School, Lucknow. Five rounds of structured debate. Four elite committees. 200+ delegates. One crucible.

Our Vision

Reason. Reform. Rise.

Jaipuria Symposium 2026 is an inter-school Model UN and academic debate event designed for students of Classes 8 through 12. Over two days, delegates navigate real-world crises, draft landmark resolutions, and forge connections that last beyond the chambers.

The Symposium runs five rounds of debate across four committees — moving from Oxford Style in Round 1 through Turncoat, Quarter-Finals, Semi-Finals, and the Grand Final. Every round escalates in intensity, format, and stakes.

This is not just a competition — it is a crucible.

The Format

Five Rounds. One Champion.

Every team competes through the same five-round structure. The format changes each round — testing breadth, adaptability, and depth.

01
Oxford Style Debate
Day 1 · All Committees

Two speakers per side (1 for, 1 against) plus one interjector. 4 minutes per speaker, 1-minute open rebuttal. The top 5 teams per committee advance to Round 2.

02
Turncoat Debate
Day 1 · All Committees

Two speakers per team. Each speaks for 6 minutes — 3 minutes arguing for a position, then a bell, then 3 minutes arguing against it. Teams are distributed across 4 pools. Top 2 per pool qualify.

03
Quarter-Finals
Day 2 · 8 Teams

Two parallel forums of 4 teams each — the U.S. Executive Leadership Debate and the British Governance Conclave. 2 speakers per team, 4.5 minutes each. One winner per forum advances.

04
Semi-Finals
Day 2 · 4 Teams

3v3 format across two simultaneous venues. Principal (5 min), Second (4 min), Third (4 min), Principal summing-up (4 min). 2-minute rebuttals follow each speaker.

05
Grand Final
Day 2 · 2 Teams

The same 3v3 format as the Semi-Finals, with the full weight of the Symposium watching. One team rises.
